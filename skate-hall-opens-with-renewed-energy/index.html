December 8, 2019

Skate hall opens with renewed energy

It will be a renovated and updated skating rink that welcomes guests when there is a faceoff on the new season on Saturday, October 12.

Aarhus Skøjtehal has changed him over the summer. When the old ice skating rink from 1964 opens for the season on Saturday, it will be in a much more sustainable version than previously with insulated flooring as well as new energy-saving cooling and ventilation systems.


“The skating rink has been a boost to the benefit of both users and the environment. Thus, the renovation lives up to two of the things that are most important to me. We need to strengthen the framework and the healthy communities and sport of the community, and we must ensure a more sustainable future, ”says Rabih Azad-Ahmad, Minister of Culture and Citizens Service.


It's a bigger game the skating rink has been through. The floor of the ice rink itself has been insulated to such an extent that energy consumption is expected to be halved.



The ice sheet itself is also halved. In the future it is only 3-5 cm. thick against previous 8-12 cm. This means that in the future, less resources will be spent on preparing the ice and much less energy on keeping the ice frozen.

In addition, the old refrigeration system has been replaced to a more climate friendly, and a new modern ventilation system has been installed in the hall.

As an added bonus, the renovation work has also provided a more pleasant experience for the visitors. Radiant heating panels have been installed in the ceiling so that the heat can be turned on over the spectator seats and the old worn wooden band around the ice rink has been replaced to a more modern model.



The renovation has cost about DKK 20 million.

The renovated ice rink can be experienced from Saturday, October 12 at noon, when the doors open for the season.
